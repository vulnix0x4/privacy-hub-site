> **TL;DR.** The attackers come in three tiers: phishers, exchange-breach re-users, and physical-attack (wrench) operators. Your priority is to make the wrench-attack operator's OSINT cost too high to justify targeting you specifically. That means scrubbing the public-records layer, separating crypto-identity from real-name identity, and keeping the size of your holdings out of search-indexable space.

## Threat picture

The wrench attack is not a meme. There have been dozens of on-record cases of people being held at gunpoint or kidnapped after identifying themselves as crypto-rich online. The prerequisite for this attack is that the attacker can find you. Your goal: ensure that finding you requires more investigation than your holdings justify.

## What you get from this playlist

Identity compartmentalization: your real name nowhere near your wallet addresses, your home address scrubbed from data brokers, your seed phrase on an encrypted volume, your exchange accounts behind hardware 2FA. Not invisibility. Just enough friction.

## Notes from real people

Every long-term self-custodian will tell you the same thing: do not tell people IRL what you hold. The wrench attack almost always starts with a conversation at a bar or a Twitter reply. Speak in ranges or not at all.

## What we don't cover here

On-chain privacy tools (mixers, privacy chains, coinjoin). Those are both jurisdictionally fraught and change monthly. Consult a tax professional in your jurisdiction before using any of them; some US services (Tornado Cash) carry OFAC sanctions exposure.
