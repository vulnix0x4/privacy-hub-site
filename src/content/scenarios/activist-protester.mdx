> **TL;DR.** You want to coordinate, document, and show up without handing law enforcement (or hostile counter-movements) a searchable dossier. Priorities: a locked phone with a strong passcode, Signal with short disappearance timers, and no biometric unlock while you are on the street.

## Threat picture

Ranges enormously. At one end: local PD with stingrays, body cams, and a licence plate reader at the rally point. At the other: intelligence agencies running network surveillance at protests of scale. Counter-movements with their own OSINT teams are now routine. Your phone is the richest single piece of evidence a hostile agency can obtain from you.

## What you get from this playlist

A baseline "do not turn yourself into trivial evidence" posture. Hardened phone, ephemeral messaging, separated social identities, tunnelled traffic. Nothing illegal-advising; nothing silver-bullet.

## Notes from real people

Organizers say turn off biometrics before the march. US case law allows police to compel Face ID or Touch ID in many jurisdictions; they cannot compel a memorized passcode (5th Amendment). Know your legal observers' numbers by heart, not just saved.

## What we don't cover here

Operational security at actual protests: buddy systems, medic plans, legal-observer coordination. Electronic Frontier Foundation's "Surveillance Self-Defense" protest guide is the starting point; the National Lawyers Guild publishes jurisdiction-specific know-your-rights cards.
