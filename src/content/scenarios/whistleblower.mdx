> **TL;DR.** Do not do anything technical until you have spoken to a lawyer. The Government Accountability Project (GAP) and the Project on Government Oversight (POGO) are experienced in whistleblower intake and can advise on legal protections before you act. The Whistleblower Protection Act (US) and equivalents elsewhere have specific procedural requirements that matter enormously to your outcome.

## Threat picture

Corporate internal-investigations teams subpoena Slack, Google Workspace, badge logs, and MDM device telemetry as a matter of course when they suspect an internal leak. Federal leaks add signals-intelligence investigation. The most common mistake is using a work device or a work-network-connected personal device to contact a journalist; that single action is usually enough for a forensic team to identify you.

## What you get from this playlist

A separated communications plane that does not touch your employer's network, their devices, or their identity providers. A clean phone, a personal email on a different provider than your work mail, a VPN that your employer did not set up.

## Notes from real people

Former whistleblowers who did it legally emphasize that a lawyer's first call is the single most protective act. GAP (https://whistleblower.org) and POGO (https://pogo.org) both refer pro-bono counsel. If/When/How, in its broader scope, also maintains legal resources.

## What we don't cover here

Anything legally advisory. Specifically: whether any particular disclosure is protected under your jurisdiction, whether internal channels are required first, and what immunity you may or may not have. Consult a lawyer before you act. This site does not replace that call.
