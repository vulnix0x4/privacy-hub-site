> **TL;DR.** The Trevor Project's crisis line is 1-866-488-7386 and they take text and chat. That is the most important number on this page. The rest is how to keep your existence online from ending up as a screenshot held up at the dinner table.

## Threat picture

Household actors with administrative access to the WiFi, sometimes the device, sometimes the Apple ID or Google account, and almost always more informal surveillance (walking past the screen, picking up an unlocked phone). The goal is to reduce the surface area they can look at without having to lie outright about using a phone.

## What you get from this playlist

A compartmentalized second identity that lives on your phone: different email, different social, different chat history. The family-visible layer stays boring. Your real communications live in Signal with disappearing messages, and nothing survives a phone scroll.

## Notes from real people

Kids who have done this say the decisive move is a password-locked phone with biometric disabled. In a hostile household, biometrics get compelled. A long memorized passcode does not.

## What we don't cover here

Legal rights as a minor. Those vary enormously by state and country. The Trevor Project's hotline (1-866-488-7386) and Lambda Legal (https://lambdalegal.org) are both places with staff who know the specifics of your jurisdiction. Call them; do not try to work out your state's law from a forum post.
