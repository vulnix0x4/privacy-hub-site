> **TL;DR.** This is not a do-it-yourself project. Work with an advocate. The National Domestic Violence Hotline at 1-800-799-7233 has a team trained in exactly this. They will walk you through device triage and will know the local shelters and legal-aid resources.

## Threat picture

An abusive partner often has administrative access to shared devices, router, cloud accounts, the kids' devices, and the car's companion app. Modern cars share location with whoever is on the account. "Find My" networks cover AirTags hidden in a bag. Stalkerware is commercial and installed easily. Assume anything you have ever shared is readable by them.

## What you get from this playlist

A clean-break posture: a new device, a new email, new passwords, new payment rails. Not invisibility, which may be out of reach. Enough separation to receive services, hold a job, and communicate with a lawyer without the partner detecting or disrupting it.

## Notes from real people

Advocates say the single most common mistake is changing passwords on the old phone. The stalkerware app captures the new password immediately. Use a clean device from the first moment, and assume the old device is compromised until an advocate helps you triage it.

## What we don't cover here

Custody, legal protective orders, or shelter logistics. Those go through your advocate and your lawyer. The Safety Net project at NNEDV (https://nnedv.org/content/safety-net) publishes technology-safety resources specifically for survivor services.
