> **TL;DR.** Someone who likes your content too much has figured out how to find you. You cannot quit the internet; you can make finding you cost more than they will pay. Priority: strip the public-records layer, then layer VPN and masked payments over every device that touches your real identity.

## Threat picture

A determined individual with your handle, probably some of your videos, and a willingness to spend hours on OSINT. They do not have state resources. They do have time and the open web's data-broker layer. Most stalking incidents escalate when the target becomes reachable at a specific physical address; your single most valuable move is making that address cost $2000 of private-investigator time rather than $9.99 of BeenVerified.

## What you get from this playlist

A clean separation between the persona your stalker follows and the real-world address, phone, and inbox where bills and court mail arrive. Not anonymity, which you cannot have as a public creator. Compartmentalization.

## Notes from real people

Creators who have been through this say the hardest part is realizing that the platform's "report" button does almost nothing. Document everything, preserve evidence with timestamps, and build a relationship with local law enforcement before you need one.

## What we don't cover here

Active platform-side abuse (harassment campaigns, mass reports). That is a Trust & Safety team problem, not a privacy problem. If you are being swatted or doxxed, the Crash Override Network (https://crashoverridenetwork.com) is a better starting point than any tool on this site.
