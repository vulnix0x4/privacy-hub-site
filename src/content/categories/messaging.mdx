> **TL;DR.** Use Signal. End-to-end encrypted by default, open-source, minimal metadata retention, and everyone already has it or will install it when you ask. Phone-number requirement is the one compromise; usernames are now available as a partial mitigation.

## What this category protects

Message content from the platform operator, from lawful intercept, from data breaches. Sometimes metadata too — who talked to whom, when, and for how long. Different messengers make different tradeoffs here.

## Hero pick: Signal

Signal Protocol is the gold standard; every major "encrypted" messenger copied it. Encrypts content and a lot of metadata (sealed sender). Open-source clients and server. Funded by a non-profit foundation. What you give up: you need a phone number to sign up, even if you hide it afterward behind a username.

## Alternatives

**SimpleX Chat** — The only mainstream messenger with no user identifier at all; every conversation uses a unique queue ID. Signal-quality encryption. Smaller network, less polished UX.

**Session** — No phone number, no email, decentralized onion-routed transport. Slower and smaller network than Signal. Popular with the more paranoid end of the spectrum.

**Wire** — Swiss/German, supports email signup, designed for business with conference calls and shared spaces. Free personal tier is usable; paid tiers target companies.
