> **TL;DR.** Run Mullvad (or any audited flat-fee VPN) on your router instead of on every device. One config, every phone and laptop and smart-TV on the LAN gets covered, including the guest devices that never would have run a client.

## What this category protects

Background leakage. Steam updaters, Spotify telemetry, the smart-TV that phones home every minute, the Roomba, the kids' switch — any device you forgot about ships DNS and traffic straight to your ISP otherwise.

## Hero pick: Mullvad on a compatible router

Any router that supports OpenWRT or a WireGuard config. Load Mullvad's WireGuard config (their website generates it for you), force every outbound connection through the tunnel. What you give up: 10-20 percent of your LAN throughput in most setups, a full weekend if you have never touched router firmware.

## Alternatives

**Proton VPN on router** — Same approach with Proton. Worth it if you already pay Proton for everything else and want one vendor.

**GL.iNet pre-built routers** — Ship with a WireGuard client UI and a pre-cut onboarding flow. The "Slate AX" and "Flint 2" boxes are popular. You pay for the convenience; the unit is yours.

**pfSense or OPNsense self-hosted** — For homelabbers. Full firewall, per-client routing rules, kill switch. Runs on any old x86 machine. Steepest learning curve.
