> **TL;DR.** Use Proton Mail. It encrypts messages at rest, does not scan your inbox for ad targeting, and supports aliases out of the box through SimpleLogin. Free tier is enough for a primary inbox; the paid plan adds custom domains.

## What this category protects

Your identity across every account you have ever created. Email is the reset-password master key for your entire digital life. A breach at your provider cascades; so does a policy of selling ad-targeting metadata based on the subject lines.

## Hero pick: Proton Mail

Zero-access encryption (Proton cannot read the body of your messages). Based in Switzerland, under Swiss data protection law, audited annually. Bundles with Proton Drive, Proton VPN, and SimpleLogin if you want one vendor for everything. What you give up: some IMAP clients need a bridge app, and the free tier limits storage more than Gmail does.

## Alternatives

**Tutanota** — German provider, full end-to-end encryption including subject lines (Proton leaves subjects in the clear). Downside: no IMAP, so you live in their app.

**Mailbox.org** — €1/month, German, open-standards-first. You get IMAP, CalDAV, CardDAV, and a minimal web UI. Pick this if you prefer to self-integrate with Thunderbird.

**Posteo** — €1/month, German, powered entirely by renewable energy if that matters to you. No custom domains on the cheapest tier.
