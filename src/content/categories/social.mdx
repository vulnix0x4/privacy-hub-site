> **TL;DR.** Use Bluesky if you want a Twitter replacement that just works and lets you move your identity to a different server later. Use Mastodon or a Fediverse instance if you want the actual decentralized future.

## What this category protects

Your social graph from ad-targeting enrichment, and your posts from being training data for whoever buys the platform next. Federated or bring-your-own-host social media replaces platform lock-in with protocol portability.

## Hero pick: Bluesky or the Fediverse

Bluesky uses the AT Protocol: you can move your handle to another provider later, take your followers, no rehosting required. Fediverse (ActivityPub) is older, larger, and more heterogeneous. Both beat staying on X, Instagram, or Threads. What you give up: smaller audiences for some communities, federation-lag quirks, and the mental tax of picking a home server on the Fediverse.

## Alternatives

**Mastodon** — The Fediverse flagship. Pick a well-run instance (mastodon.social is the default but larger ones have looser moderation). Works with Pleroma, Misskey, and other ActivityPub software.

**Pixelfed** — Federated Instagram replacement. Small but real. Good if you mostly share photos.

**Matrix** — Not strictly social media, but a federated protocol for group chat that competes with Discord. Good for communities that want to own their archive.
