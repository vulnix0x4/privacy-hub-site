> **TL;DR.** Use Ente. End-to-end encrypted photo library with excellent mobile apps, face recognition done client-side, 5 GB free, paid plans competitive with Google Photos. Works on iOS, Android, web, and desktop.

## What this category protects

Your photo library is location history (EXIF GPS), face recognition fodder, lifestyle profiling, and child-safety-scan input all in one bucket. Google Photos scans every image. iCloud Photos scans some. Encrypted photo services do not.

## Hero pick: Ente

Zero-knowledge. Face recognition runs on your device, the server never sees faces. Open-source clients. Family plan at $24/year for 200 GB beats Google's family pricing. What you give up: sharing albums externally requires a recipient-side decryption step; smooth but one more tap.

## Alternatives

**Proton Drive** — Photos are first-class in Proton Drive's mobile app now. Good if you want one vendor for files and photos and already pay Proton.

**iCloud with Advanced Data Protection** — Apple's zero-knowledge tier. Requires iOS 16+ and switching on ADP (Settings -&gt; Apple ID -&gt; iCloud -&gt; Advanced Data Protection). Best if you live in the Apple ecosystem.

**Immich (self-hosted)** — The open-source Google Photos clone. Runs on a home server. Best if you want full control and do not mind maintaining infrastructure.
