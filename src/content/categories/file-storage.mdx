> **TL;DR.** Use Proton Drive. End-to-end encrypted, integrated with the rest of the Proton suite, and cheap. The free tier gives 5 GB, plenty for documents; paid plans start at €4/month for 200 GB.

## What this category protects

Your documents, photos, backups, and anything else you upload. With Dropbox or Google Drive, the provider has the keys and can hand over plaintext on subpoena, scan for policy violations, or use content to train AI. With end-to-end encryption, they cannot.

## Hero pick: Proton Drive

Swiss jurisdiction, zero-access encryption, open-source clients. File sharing uses cryptographic per-link keys rather than just a URL guess. What you give up: no block-level sync yet (every file change re-uploads the whole file), which matters if you work with large binary assets all day.

## Alternatives

**Tresorit** — Swiss, business-focused, premium pricing (~€10/month personal). Best if you need zero-knowledge team folders with granular permissions.

**Filen** — German, cheap, genuinely end-to-end encrypted, younger company than the others. Pick if cost matters and you do not need polish.

**MEGA** — New Zealand, 20 GB free, end-to-end encrypted. Controversial founder history, but the crypto is real. Generous free tier.
