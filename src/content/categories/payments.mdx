> **TL;DR.** Use Privacy.com (US only). Free. Funds from your bank, creates a unique virtual card per merchant, lock-to-merchant so a breach cannot charge anywhere else. If you live outside the US, Revolut's disposable virtual cards are the closest equivalent.

## What this category protects

The flow of your real card number across the web. Every time you paste your card into a checkout, the number lives in that merchant's PCI scope, their risk vendor's logs, their bot-management vendor's model, and eventually their breach. A per-merchant virtual card contains the blast radius to one site.

## Hero pick: Privacy.com

Free for up to 12 cards a month. Each card can be single-use or merchant-locked with a spend cap. US bank required. What you give up: US only; international coverage is not on the roadmap.

## Alternatives

**Revolut virtual cards** — Disposable virtual cards rotate per use, works across Europe, UK, and Australia. Free tier includes five disposable cards per month.

**Monero** — Actual cryptocurrency with native privacy guarantees (ring signatures, stealth addresses, confidential amounts). Use for vendors that accept it directly; acquisition without KYC has gotten harder.

**Cash** — Still the most private payment method. Works in person, no digital trail, refused by most online merchants but still a live option for many things.
