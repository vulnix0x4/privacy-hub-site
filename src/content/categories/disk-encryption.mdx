> **TL;DR.** Use VeraCrypt if you want cross-platform, open-source full-disk encryption for a data drive or a removable volume. For your OS volume, use whatever ships with the OS (FileVault on macOS, BitLocker on Windows Pro, LUKS on most Linux distros) — it is convenient, properly integrated, and adequate for anything short of a nation-state adversary.

## What this category protects

Your data against physical theft, sloppy pawnshop disposal, and border crossings with sleeping devices. Without disk encryption, anyone who boots from a USB stick reads your drive.

## Hero pick: VeraCrypt

Open-source, audited (OSTIF sponsored the 2016 audit), runs on every desktop OS. Encrypts whole volumes or creates encrypted containers that look like ordinary files. Supports plausible-deniability hidden volumes if your threat model genuinely needs them. What you give up: no mobile clients (use the OS tools there), and it does not integrate with secure-boot as cleanly as BitLocker.

## Alternatives

**BitLocker** — Ships with Windows Pro, uses the TPM so no password required at boot. Good default on Windows; just confirm your recovery key is saved somewhere other than the drive itself.

**FileVault** — macOS default. On by default on Apple Silicon. Tied to your iCloud account unless you deliberately choose a local recovery key.

**LUKS** — Linux standard. Most distros offer it at install time. No reason not to tick the box.
