> **TL;DR.** Use GrapheneOS on a Pixel. Best-in-class Android security hardening, strictly opt-in Google Play services in a sandbox, and no relationship with Google as the OS vendor. Requires a Pixel 8 or newer.

## What this category protects

The thousand ambient telemetry streams your phone OS produces. Android pings Google every few minutes with location, device info, and behavioral signals unless you disable it — and a lot of it cannot be disabled. iOS does the same with Apple, though the flavor is different.

## Hero pick: GrapheneOS

Hardened fork of AOSP. Sandboxes Google Play services as a regular app with no special permissions (most apps still work). Hardware-enforced verified boot, hardened memory allocator, strict permission model. What you give up: Pixel hardware only, one-time 20-minute install, and some banking apps that block GrapheneOS's attestation (use their web app or a separate profile).

## Alternatives

**CalyxOS** — Sister project, similar threat model, slightly different tradeoffs (includes MicroG by default). Pick if you want Google-API compatibility without GrapheneOS's strict sandbox approach.

**Hardened iOS** — Stock iOS with Lockdown Mode, Advanced Data Protection, and private relay turned on. Convenience floor is high; ceiling is lower than GrapheneOS.

**LineageOS** — More device coverage than Graphene or Calyx, less security hardening. Pick if your phone is not a recent Pixel and you cannot upgrade.
