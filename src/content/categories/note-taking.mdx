> **TL;DR.** Use Standard Notes if you want phone-and-desktop synced notes that are end-to-end encrypted by default. Free tier is plain-text only; paid tier unlocks rich editors and 2FA.

## What this category protects

Sync convenience without sync surveillance. Evernote, Notion, OneNote, and Google Keep all store your notes plaintext on their servers. A sync-aware encrypted app keeps the convenience and removes the provider's read access.

## Hero pick: Standard Notes

Zero-knowledge, end-to-end encrypted, cross-platform. Free tier syncs plaintext notes; the $90/year Productivity plan adds Markdown, code, spreadsheet, and task-list editors. Minimal by design. What you give up: no realtime collaboration; sharing is per-note export.

## Alternatives

**Joplin** — Self-sync via Nextcloud, WebDAV, S3, or Joplin Cloud. Markdown-native. Best for people who already run a cloud they trust.

**Obsidian Sync** — Obsidian's first-party sync, $4/month, end-to-end encrypted. Pick if Obsidian is already your notes app and you want zero-config sync.

**Cryptee** — End-to-end encrypted documents and photos. EU-hosted, minimal UI. Useful for mixed document-plus-photo journaling.
