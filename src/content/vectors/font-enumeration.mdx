> **TL;DR.** Your installed-font list reflects every program you have ever installed: Microsoft Office adds dozens, Adobe adds hundreds, every game adds a few. The list is high-entropy and stable.

A script does not need a permission prompt to enumerate it. There are at least three reliable techniques and browsers patch them slowly.

## How it works

The classic method: render a string in a known fallback font, measure the bounding box, then render it in the candidate font and measure again. If the boxes differ, the candidate font is installed. Loop over a list of 5,000 candidate fonts and you get a per-machine bitmap.

The CSS Font Loading API offers a faster route. The newer `queryLocalFonts()` requires permission, which is the right design and is why most fingerprinters still use the measurement trick.

## What it leaks about you

Your OS, your locale (CJK fonts give you away), every Office or design suite you have installed, sometimes specific niche software (game fonts, accessibility fonts).

## How to defend

- 🟢 Use Tor Browser, which restricts fonts to a hardcoded shortlist.
- 🟢 Firefox FPP returns a fixed font list to scripts that probe.
- 🟡 In Brave, fingerprinting protection farbles font measurements per session.
- 🔴 On Linux, run a containerized browser (Bubblewrap, Flatpak) with only its bundled fonts.

## What doesn't help

Uninstalling fonts you do not use. Even small lists are high-entropy and your OS-shipped defaults are not uniform across versions.
