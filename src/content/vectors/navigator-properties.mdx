> **TL;DR.** `navigator.hardwareConcurrency`, `navigator.deviceMemory`, `navigator.plugins`, `navigator.languages`, `navigator.maxTouchPoints`, `navigator.cookieEnabled`, `navigator.pdfViewerEnabled`, `navigator.connection`. Each leaks 1-3 bits. Together they leak you.

This is the long tail of fingerprinting: dozens of innocuous properties, summed.

## How it works

Every property is a getter. A script reads them in a loop, concatenates, hashes. CPU core count narrows you to "8-core machines" (still many millions, but a big cut). Device memory rounds to powers of two but reflects the real machine. Connection type ("4g", "wifi") plus downlink and rtt round to discrete buckets that change with network.

Most are not protected by a permission prompt. Most are stable across reloads.

## What it leaks about you

CPU core count, RAM tier, GPU presence (via plugins on some platforms), whether you can do touch, your declared language preferences, whether your connection is metered.

## How to defend

- 🟢 Tor Browser and Mullvad Browser return uniform values for all of these.
- 🟢 Firefox FPP normalizes hardwareConcurrency and deviceMemory to standard values.
- 🟡 Brave lies about specific high-entropy properties (deviceMemory rounds to 8, hardwareConcurrency to 2).

## What doesn't help

Disabling JavaScript on the page; you turn it back on for the next site and re-leak the same values.
