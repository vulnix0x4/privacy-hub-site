> **TL;DR.** A site asks WebGL `getParameter(UNMASKED_RENDERER_WEBGL)` and gets back something like "ANGLE (NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3070 Direct3D11)". That string plus the OpenGL extension list narrows you to a handful of machines on the planet.

Combined with canvas fingerprinting, this is the single most damaging passive identifier in the modern web.

## How it works

WebGL exposes a debug extension named `WEBGL_debug_renderer_info` that returns the unmasked GPU vendor and renderer string. The full list of supported extensions, max texture size, max render buffer size, anisotropy support, and shading-language version all add entropy. Then a script renders a 3D scene off-screen and hashes the pixels, which is canvas fingerprinting on hard mode (more variation per machine).

Cloudflare, Akamai, FingerprintJS, and every modern ad-tech SDK read these. The WebGL hash plus the canvas hash is, in the open-data Panopticlick-class studies, enough to uniquely identify well over 99 percent of users.

## What it leaks about you

Your GPU, your driver version, your OS GPU stack, and indirectly your machine model (laptop vs. desktop, integrated vs. discrete graphics).

## How to defend

- 🟢 Use Tor Browser or Mullvad Browser, which return a uniform renderer string.
- 🟢 Firefox FPP (privacy.resistFingerprinting or the gentler privacy.fingerprintingProtection) farbles the WebGL parameters.
- 🟡 Disable WebGL entirely if you do not need it (about:config webgl.disabled in Firefox). Some sites break.

## What doesn't help

A VPN. Disabling JavaScript on a per-site basis (you will turn it back on for the next site and re-leak).
