> **TL;DR.** A script generates a triangle wave through a `DynamicsCompressorNode`, captures a few hundred samples, and sums or hashes them. Floating-point math, audio drivers, and CPU instruction sets perturb the output. Same machine always returns the same number.

This is one of the oldest and most reliable fingerprints. It works headless, works without speakers, works without permission.

## How it works

The Web Audio API was designed for music and games but exposes a silent `OfflineAudioContext`. A site builds a short audio graph: oscillator -&gt; compressor -&gt; analyser -&gt; nothing audible. The compressor's nonlinear math depends on the exact floating-point implementation, which depends on the CPU, the OS audio stack, and how the browser compiled the audio nodes. The output, summed to a single float, is your fingerprint.

The trick is famous because Mowery and Shacham published it in 2012. Twelve years later it still works in every browser that has not specifically farbled it.

## What it leaks about you

A stable per-machine identifier with around 5-7 bits of entropy on its own and with much more when joined to canvas, WebGL, and font enumeration.

## How to defend

- 🟢 Tor Browser and Mullvad Browser return a uniform audio fingerprint.
- 🟢 Firefox FPP and Brave both farble the AudioContext output.
- 🟡 Disabling the Web Audio API entirely breaks most modern sites; not recommended.

## What doesn't help

Muting your speakers. The audio is never played; it is computed offline.
