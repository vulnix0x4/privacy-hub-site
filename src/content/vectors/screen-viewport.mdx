> **TL;DR.** `screen.width`, `screen.height`, `screen.availWidth`, `screen.availHeight`, `window.innerWidth`, `window.innerHeight`, `window.devicePixelRatio`, `screen.colorDepth`. Together: roughly 15 bits of entropy.

The combination of "1920x1080 monitor with a 1.25 DPR and a window 1356x712" is rare enough to identify you among a few thousand peers.

## How it works

Each value is a JavaScript getter, no permission needed. Available width/height excludes the OS taskbar (Windows knows it is at the bottom; macOS knows it is at the top), so the difference between screen.height and screen.availHeight reveals your OS.

Maximizing a window leaks your taskbar setting. Resizing it constantly across the day leaks behavioral patterns.

## What it leaks about you

Monitor resolution, taskbar layout (so OS family), whether you use a high-DPR display, your window-management habits.

## How to defend

- 🟢 Tor Browser uses "letterboxing": a fixed inner window size with empty bands to hide the real one.
- 🟢 Firefox FPP rounds the inner window to 200x100 grid steps.
- 🟡 Always browse maximized; "windowed at 1247x683" is unique to you.

## What doesn't help

A VPN. The screen API is local-machine state, not network state.
