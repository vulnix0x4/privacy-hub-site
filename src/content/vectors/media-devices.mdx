> **TL;DR.** `navigator.mediaDevices.enumerateDevices()` returns the count of cameras, microphones, and speakers attached. Without permission it returns counts and types only; with permission it returns the labels (so "Logitech BRIO 4K", "AirPods Pro").

Even the unprivileged response leaks "you have three microphones, two cameras, two output devices", which is rare.

## How it works

The API was originally meant for a video-call app to populate a device picker. The early version returned full labels to anyone. Browsers later restricted labels to origins that already had `getUserMedia` permission, but the count and the deviceId hash remain readable to all sites.

The deviceId is supposed to be per-origin and per-session. In practice, several browsers leak a stable identifier across sessions until you clear site data.

## What it leaks about you

Number and type of audio/video devices. With camera/mic permission once granted, the model name. Indirectly your machine class (laptops have one camera, streamers have three).

## How to defend

- 🟢 Revoke camera and mic permission when you do not actively use them.
- 🟢 Brave and Firefox both gate labels behind a granted permission.
- 🟡 Tor Browser blocks `enumerateDevices` outright.

## What doesn't help

Disabling the camera at the OS level on Windows. The browser still sees the device exists; it just cannot stream from it.
