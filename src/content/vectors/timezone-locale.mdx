> **TL;DR.** `Intl.DateTimeFormat().resolvedOptions().timeZone` returns "America/Chicago". `navigator.languages` returns `["en-US", "es"]`. The Accept-Language header on every request announces the same. Together they put you in a city and tell every site what languages you read.

These look harmless. They are not, because they cross-reference with IP for free.

## How it works

The timezone string comes from the OS, accurate to the Olson database name (so "America/Indiana/Indianapolis" not just "GMT-5"). Languages come from the OS preferences, ordered. Accept-Language is sent on every HTTP request automatically.

A site that sees Mullvad's German exit IP plus a US Pacific timezone plus en-US,es as your languages knows you are an American on a VPN, browsing from your usual setup. No fingerprinting library needed.

## What it leaks about you

Your real city (timezone), your declared mother tongue and second languages, your immigrant status in some cases, your VPN-vs-real-location consistency.

## How to defend

- 🟢 Use Tor Browser, which forces UTC and en-US for every user.
- 🟢 Mullvad Browser does the same.
- 🟡 Firefox FPP forces the timezone to UTC and overrides Accept-Language to en-US.
- 🔴 Match your VPN exit country to your OS timezone manually if you cannot use one of the above.

## What doesn't help

Switching IP without switching timezone. The mismatch flags you as VPN-using and the timezone betrays where you actually are.
