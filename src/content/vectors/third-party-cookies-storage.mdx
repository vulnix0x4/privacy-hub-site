> **TL;DR.** When site A embeds a script or iframe from tracker.com, tracker.com can set a cookie scoped to tracker.com. Visit site B with the same tracker embed and that cookie is sent back. One identifier follows you everywhere the tracker is present.

Google's Privacy Sandbox promised to kill this in Chrome and then quietly shelved the deprecation. As of 2026 third-party cookies are still on by default for most Chrome users.

## How it works

A tracker iframe from analytics.example sets `Set-Cookie: id=abc123; SameSite=None; Secure`. Any cross-site context embedding that tracker then sends the cookie back with every request. The same trick works for `localStorage` and `IndexedDB` when the tracker is an iframe rather than a script, since each iframe gets its own partitioned-or-not storage depending on browser.

Safari blocked this in 2020 (Intelligent Tracking Prevention). Firefox did the same in 2019 (Total Cookie Protection made it default in 2022). Chrome still does not.

## What it leaks about you

A cross-site user ID. Every site you visit that embeds the same tracker learns a new intersection point on you.

## How to defend

- 🟢 Use Firefox, Brave, or Safari: all three block or partition third-party cookies by default.
- 🟢 In Chrome, turn on "Block third-party cookies" in Settings -&gt; Privacy and security. You will break a few shopping-cart flows.
- 🟡 Install uBlock Origin, which kills most tracker iframes before they can set cookies.
- 🔴 Run Pi-hole or NextDNS to null-route tracker domains at the network level.

## What doesn't help

Incognito mode. It clears cookies when you close the window but happily accepts third-party cookies during the session.
